Feeling walled in?
Change your DNS to

󰂗 Windows: Download and install automatically, or follow the directions below to manually setup

1 Open Control Panel

2 Click Network and Sharing Center

3 Change Adapter Settings

4 Right click on your network device and choose properties

5 Choose IPv4

6 Click Advanced -> DNS -> Add

 If you want to use your original settings select "Obtain DNS server address automatically"

 Mac: Download and install automatically, or follow the directions below to manually setup

1 Open System Preferences

2 Double click the Network icon

3 Double-click the connection you want to change DNS for. The connection you're using now will have a green light next to it

4 On the connection properties window, click the DNS tab

5 Use the + or - symbols to add or remove the DNS servers your connection uses. Servers will be used in the order they are shown in this list. Then click OK

 If you want to use your original settings delete the DNS addresses added in the last step

 Android: Download the app(3G supported), or manually setup following the steps below (wifi only)

1 From the Android Menu home screen, tap Settings

2 Tap Wi-Fi on the menu

3 Long press the Network you're connected to, and tap Modify Network

4 On some devices, you may need to check the box for "Advanced" to see further settings. To adjust your Android DNS settings, you will need to switch the IP settings from DHCP to Static. Once changed, the menu will appear with the DNS settings available to edit. Don't worry about the editing the IP address as the Android device will fill in the IP address it has acquired through DHCP

5 Fill in our DNS 121.201.13.176 then save your settings

 You may need to restart your phone for the configuration to take effect. To restore your old settings delete the DNS addresses added in the last step

 iPhone: Manually setup using the steps below

1 Open Settings, and tap on the wifi menu

2 Find your wifi connection and click the i to the right of the connection

3 Scroll down to the DNS section

4 Change your DNS to 121.201.13.176

 You may need to restart your phone for the configuration to take effect. To restore your old settings delete the DNS addresses added in the last step

 Routers: Change the DNS on a HiWiFi router、Change the DNS on a TPLINK router. For other routers please search for "how to change router DNS"

After configuring your router, all connected cell phones, iPads, televisions, and computers do not need to be configured to use the DNS
